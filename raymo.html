Domain: Dressing room


Raymo

Team: Esther Gatemba, Chongrui Zhao, Violet Zhao

Problem space: Lack of efficiency in Brick and Mortar retail stores.

Raymo is a smart wristlet that gives you seamless shopping experience in cloth stores. Our target users are both retail stores and their customers. A CUI is relevant to customers as they are always seeking a satisfactory shopping experience. The ease to make selections, try out, keep what they want and easily discard what they do not want to keep. While online ecommerce platforms give customers the ease to make selections, it is not convenient -even with Amazon’s 2 day shipping- to receive items bought. The efficiency of online shopping is halted when customers have to wait for a while to receive their purchase. Most buy multiple sizes and colors just to try on then they have to figure out how to send items that they do not choose back. Some never get to it and are left with clothes they do not need.

Design Considerations

The team considered the pain points that Brick and Mortar retail stores experience both from the customers' side and also from the retailers perspective. Some of these include: -Room assignment becomes a pain point for customers who have to wait in line just to get a room to try on clothes. This would be avoided and made easeir if the customers were made aware of when their rooms were ready. -Wait time is too long for a store attendant to receive a customers request from a dressing room and deliver the items that requested. With Raymo, customers get the best of both online and brick and mortar shopping. They can browse in real time, saving time in the process. Raymo eliminates the tedious aspects of the Brick and Mortar like lines at the dressing room and the cashier counter,allowing them to focus on the items that they are interested in purchasing.

Product design

Raymo: A wristelt


  • -Facial recognition connects a customers’ account to the wristlet via a sensor.
  • -Profile that has customers measurements and sizes, preference in clothes, credit card information, history of purchase and repeat.
  • -Automatically updates customer’s profile with size changes/ hold the choice information when user is in dressing room so there is no memory overload.
*the concept wristelt used in the body-storming and concept video is a wrist watch.

Final Design Product Concept

With the final design , financial constraints meant that we maximized the basic design that we created. Focusing on the straps of the shoe that could be swapped, as a team, we were able to give customers options without them having to invest in multiple shoes. For the company, the revenue is generated not only at teh initial purchase of the shoe, they get money when customers buy more generic straps or when they design their own.
