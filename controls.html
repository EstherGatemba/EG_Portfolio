Sauna Control

The goal of this project was to understand the role of feedfoward, feedback, and affordance in controld. The solution that we, as designers, needed to tackle was how to give clues of communication to users on the expected interactions with designs. With a control, the user should be able to know how to interact with the tool of control, and to understand what to expect when the control is triggered.

    Final Prototype Design

    Final Design

    The final prototype was cut using a laser cutter to achieve precision and good alignment. Additional materials used include, an orange plastic waffle cone (Construction Junction), a knob and a bulb. With this final design, there is clear affordance with the knob, feedback is given when the light turns on, the sauna heats up or the alarm sounds. The slits create a feedforward aspect to the design. This intergrated design is clean and allows different controls to exist as a single unit. This would reduce manufacturing costs. For users, the control is sleek and simple to use without a prior mental model.
