Tembea Shoe


Team: Kanika Khosla, Bryan Lum, Karthik Sudhakar, logan Uresky, Esther Gatemba

A case study project for Samaritan's Feet under the Design and Business club. The prompt for this project was to design a footwear for commercial customers. Given the following restrictions: -Must retail for less than $40. -Cost includes $20 to fund the shoe donation Samaritanâ€™s feet makes. -Must incorporate the biodegradable World shoe sole into commercial shoe for $3.50. The team proposed a customizable/DIY sandal, where the world shoe sole will be the foundation for different sandal configurations and designs that each customer can mix and match. These sandal uppers will be sustainably crafted out of recycled synthetic fibers that are biodegradable.

Design Concept

For customers who are fashion forward, wanting to match from head to toe or the customers who are interested in a small change, the multiple strap offers a 100+ range of possible matched options. The interchangeable sandal system will allow shoe lovers the ability to experiment freely with fashion in an environmentally friendly way, without the need to buy a new pair of shoes, every time they want a different style or aesthetic. A customer would receive a set of sandal top pieces that they can play with. - A wide band that connects to all four slots, 2 bands to use at the same time or with either one of the 4 thin band strips that are included in the set.

Sustainable Materials

Sustainability being a key part of this project meant that the team had to make decisions on the materials that will be used on the shoe. With this in mind, Hemp, Natural Cork, Natural Indingo Linen, Organic Cotton were decided as the materials to be used. These materials are not only Sustainable, they also have great performance in different climates that would cater to customers who live in tropical areas and those who live in colder climates.

Final Design Product Concept

With the final design , financial constraints meant that we maximized the basic design that we created. Focusing on the straps of the shoe that could be swapped, as a team, we were able to give customers options without them having to invest in multiple shoes. For the company, the revenue is generated not only at teh initial purchase of the shoe, they get money when customers buy more generic straps or when they design their own.
